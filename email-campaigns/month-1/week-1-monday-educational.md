**Subject:** Your dog isn't ignoring you. They don't understand you.

**Preview:** The real reason commands stop working outside the house.

---

Most owners think their dog "knows" the command but chooses to ignore it.

That's not what's happening.

Your dog learned "sit" in your kitchen with a treat in your hand. That's it. They learned to sit in that exact context. Change the environment, remove the treat, add a distraction, and the command doesn't exist to them anymore.

This is why group class training falls apart at the park. Why your dog listens at home but loses it on walks. Why recall works in the backyard but vanishes at the dog park.

**The fix: proof the command in the environment where you need it.**

That means practicing sit on the sidewalk. Recall at the park. Place command when guests arrive. Not in your kitchen 50 more times.

Every K9 Academy program is built around real-world proofing. We don't train in a bubble. We train in parks, streets, trails, and crowded environments so the results actually transfer to your life.

**If your dog "knows" commands but won't listen when it matters, the training method failed you. Not the other way around.**
