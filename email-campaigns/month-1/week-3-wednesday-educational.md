**Subject:** 5 things you're doing that make reactivity worse

**Preview:** Number 3 is the one most owners don't realize.

---

If your dog is reactive on leash, you're probably doing at least one of these without realizing it.

**1. Tightening the leash when you see another dog.**
Your dog feels the tension. Tension = there's something to worry about. You're confirming the threat before the dog even reacts.

**2. Saying "it's okay" over and over.**
Your dog doesn't understand English. They hear your anxious tone. That confirms something is wrong.

**3. Crossing the street every time.**
You're teaching your dog that other dogs are something to avoid. Avoidance reinforces fear.

**4. Using treats to distract mid-reaction.**
If your dog is already lunging and barking, a treat isn't training. It's bribery. And it rewards the arousal state.

**5. Letting other people approach your dog "to socialize."**
Forcing interaction on a reactive dog doesn't socialize them. It floods them. It makes it worse.

**What to do instead:**

Create distance before the reaction starts. Reward calm behaviour, not just the absence of barking. And work with a trainer who understands balanced methods, not just positive reinforcement.
