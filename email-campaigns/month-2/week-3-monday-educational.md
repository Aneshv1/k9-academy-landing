**Subject:** Your dog doesn't need more exercise. They need more structure.

**Preview:** (leave blank for plain-text)

---

Hot take from me today.

I see owners running their dogs for two hours a day trying to "tire them out." Then they wonder why the dog is still jumping on guests, pulling on leash, and destroying the house.

Here's the truth. A tired dog is not a trained dog. You're building an athlete who needs more and more exercise to reach the same level of calm. That's a treadmill you'll never get off.

What actually calms a dog down is structure.

Place command for 30 minutes while you eat dinner. Crate rest between activities. Structured walks where the dog walks beside you instead of dragging you wherever they want to go. Thresholds at every door.

I've seen dogs go from "needs three hours at the dog park" to completely calm in the house after two weeks of structure. No extra exercise. Just rules, boundaries, and consistency.

If your dog is "high energy" and nothing seems to help, the answer probably isn't more fetch. It's more structure.

Try this: one week of enforced crate naps, structured meal times, and place command practice. Watch what happens.
