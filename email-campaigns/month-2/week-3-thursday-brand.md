**Subject:** Why I started K9 Academy

**Preview:** (leave blank for plain-text)

---

People ask me this a lot so I figured I'd just tell the story.

I didn't grow up wanting to be a dog trainer. I grew up around dogs, always had them, but it wasn't the plan.

What happened was I got a dog that was out of control. A German Shepherd that was reactive, wouldn't listen, and made every walk a nightmare. I hired a trainer. Then another one. Then a third. None of them fixed the problem. They all had different methods, different philosophies, and none of it worked in the real world.

So I started learning myself. Read everything I could. Worked with mentors. Spent years figuring out what actually works and what's just theory that sounds good in a classroom.

Eventually people started asking me to help with their dogs. Then their friends' dogs. Then it turned into a business.

K9 Academy started because I couldn't find what I was looking for as a dog owner. So I built it. A training program that delivers real results, in real environments, with real accountability.

Fifteen years later we've trained thousands of dogs across two facilities. And the mission hasn't changed. Train the dog for the life the owner actually lives.

That's it. That's the whole story.

Anesh
