**Subject:** The 3 commands every dog must know before going off leash

**Preview:** If your dog can't do these three things, they're not ready.

---

Off leash freedom is the goal for most dog owners. But letting your dog off leash before they're ready is how dogs get lost, hit by cars, or start fights at the park.

Before your dog goes off leash anywhere, they need three commands to be bulletproof:

**1. Recall ("Come")**
Not "come... come... COME HERE." One command, instant response, every time. In any environment. With any distraction. If your dog doesn't come the first time you call, they're not ready for off leash.

**2. Place**
Your dog needs to be able to go to a spot and stay there until released. This is your emergency brake. Dog sees a squirrel, a cyclist, another dog? "Place" stops them in their tracks.

**3. Leave It**
This covers everything from food on the ground to other animals. Your dog needs to disengage from whatever has their attention on your command. Not after thinking about it. Immediately.

**All three need to work at distance, under distraction, and without a leash to enforce them.**

That's the standard. Anything less and you're gambling with your dog's safety.
