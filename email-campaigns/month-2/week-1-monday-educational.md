**Subject:** Why your dog doesn't listen off leash

**Preview:** It's not stubbornness. It's context-dependent learning.

---

Your dog sits perfectly in the kitchen. Comes when called in the backyard. But off leash at the park? Gone. Like they've never heard your voice before.

Here's why.

Dogs learn in context. "Come" at home and "come" at the park are two completely different commands to your dog. Different smells, sounds, and distractions mean the behavior doesn't transfer automatically. Your dog isn't being stubborn. They literally haven't learned the command in that environment yet.

**This is why e-collar training works so well for off-leash reliability.**

An e-collar gives you a communication tool that works at any distance, in any environment. It bridges the gap between what your dog knows at home and what they can do in the real world. When conditioned properly, it's not punishment. It's a conversation.

Every off-leash dog you see walking calmly beside their owner at a park didn't get there through treats and hope. They got there through structured proofing in real environments.
