**Subject:** Private lessons vs group classes. My honest take.

**Preview:** (leave blank for plain-text)

---

I get this question every week so here's my honest answer.

Group classes are great if your dog is social, not reactive, and you want to learn alongside other owners. You get structured curriculum, real-world distractions built into the class, and it's more affordable. Level 1 is $595. The L1+L2 bundle is $995. For most puppies and dogs without major behavioral issues, group classes are the right call.

Private lessons are better if your dog has specific issues that need one-on-one attention. Reactivity, aggression, severe anxiety, or any problem that would make a group class setting stressful for your dog or disruptive for others. A 6-session package is $1,350. An 8-session package is $1,685.

Here's what I tell people: if your dog can be around other dogs without losing it, start with group classes. If they can't, start with privates and transition to group later.

There's no upsell here. I'd rather put you in the right program from day one than sell you the expensive option and have it be the wrong fit.

If you're not sure which one your dog needs, reply to this email. Tell me what's going on and I'll give you a straight answer.
