**Subject:** "My wife and I just stared at each other"

**Preview:** (leave blank for plain-text)

---

I got an email from a client named Sarah a few weeks after her dog Miles finished our 4-week Board and Train.

She said when they picked Miles up and brought him home, he walked through the front door and went straight to his place bed. Laid down. Didn't move.

Her two kids were running around the house. The other dog was barking. Miles just stayed on his bed, calm, watching everything.

She wrote: "My wife and I just stared at each other. We didn't say anything for a full minute. We couldn't believe this was the same dog that used to jump on everyone who walked through the door."

Before training, Miles would bolt out of the house if the door opened. He knocked over their youngest twice. He would counter surf, steal food off the table, and bark nonstop when left alone.

Four weeks later he was a different dog.

That email is why I do this. Not every client writes to tell you. But when they do, it hits different.

If your dog is running your household instead of the other way around, reply to this email. I'll point you in the right direction.
