**Subject:** A day in the life at our Stouffville facility

**Preview:** Here's what your dog's week actually looks like during Board & Train.

---

People ask what their dog does all day during a Board and Train. Fair question. Here's the actual daily schedule at our Stouffville facility:

**6:30 AM -Morning turnout.**
Dogs go out for a supervised yard session. Bathroom break, stretch, sniff around.

**7:30 AM -Breakfast + crate time.**
Structured feeding. Dogs eat in their crates to build calm mealtime habits.

**9:00 AM -Training session 1.**
45-60 minutes of focused obedience. Commands, leash work, impulse control. One-on-one with a trainer.

**11:00 AM -Socialization or field trip.**
Controlled exposure to new environments. Parks, trails, pet stores, busy sidewalks.

**1:00 PM -Rest.**
Dogs need downtime to process what they've learned. Crate rest with enrichment.

**3:00 PM -Training session 2.**
Another 45-60 minutes. We work on different skills or proof what was taught in the morning session.

**5:00 PM -Dinner + evening turnout.**
Structured feeding again, followed by yard time and decompression.

**7:00 PM -Settle for the night.**

**This is 3-4 hours of structured training per day.** Most "board and trains" give your dog 20 minutes and call it a day. We don't operate like that.
