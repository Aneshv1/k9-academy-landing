**Subject:** The rescue that wouldn't look at anyone

**Preview:** (leave blank for plain-text)

---

Last year I worked with a rescue named Nala. Pit bull mix, about three years old. Spent the first two years of her life in a crate in someone's basement.

When she arrived she wouldn't look at me. Wouldn't look at anyone. Tail tucked, body pressed against the wall. If you reached toward her she would shut down completely. Just freeze.

The first four days I didn't try to train her at all. I just sat near her crate and let her exist. Day five she sniffed my hand. Day seven she took a treat from my palm.

By week two she was walking on a leash. Not perfectly. But walking.

By week four she was doing place command on the dog bed in our facility. Relaxed. Tail up for the first time since she arrived.

She's in a home now with two other dogs. Her owner sends me videos of her playing in the yard. Mouth open, ears back, full zoomies.

Some dogs don't need obedience first. They need someone to show them the world isn't going to hurt them anymore. Then the obedience comes easy.

That case reminded me that training isn't always about commands. Sometimes it's just about trust.
