### 2. Reviews — Keep Them Coming

250+ at 4.9 is excellent. But Google favours recency. A business with 250 reviews from 2 years ago ranks lower than one with 150 reviews from the last 6 months.

**Action items:**
- [ ] Add a review request to your post-training email flow (Klaviyo)
- [ ] Text/email clients 1 week after program completion asking for a Google review
- [ ] Include a direct Google review link (search "Google review link generator")
- [ ] Respond to EVERY review — positive and negative. Google tracks owner responses.
- [ ] Target: 5-10 new reviews per month minimum

### 3. Local Citations — Consistent NAP

NAP = Name, Address, Phone. Google cross-references your business info across the web. Inconsistencies hurt rankings.

**Action items:**
- [ ] List on: Yelp, Yellow Pages Canada, BBB, Foursquare, Hotfrog, Cylex, 411.ca
- [ ] Ensure identical NAP everywhere: "K9 Academy Dog Training Center" / "16-30 Canvarco Rd, Toronto, ON M4G 1L3" / "437-778-5273"
- [ ] List on dog-specific directories: DogTrainingDirectory.ca, PetFriendly.ca, BlogTO (pet section)
- [ ] List on local directories: BlogTO, Toronto.com, Leaside BIA, East York BIA

### 4. Backlinks — Build Authority

Backlinks from relevant sites tell Google you're a real authority. Quality over quantity.

**Action items:**
- [ ] Reach out to Toronto bloggers who write about pets/dogs — offer a guest post or interview
