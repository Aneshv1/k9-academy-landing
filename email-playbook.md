**Subject:** Stop saying your dog's name when they do something wrong
**Preview:** You're accidentally poisoning their recall

> Sound familiar? Here's the problem: every time you say your dog's name in a frustrated or angry tone, you're teaching them that their name means something bad is about to happen.

### Campaign 7 — May 21 (Wednesday)
**Type:** Training Tip
**Subject:** The "place" command will change your life
**Preview:** 15 minutes to teach, a lifetime of calm

> Hi {{ person.first_name|default:"there" }},
>
> If you could only teach your dog one thing, we'd say teach "place."
>
> **What it is:** Your dog goes to a specific spot (bed, mat, cot) and stays there until released.
>
> **Why it matters:** It replaces jumping on guests, begging at the table, following you around the house, and 90% of the annoying behaviours owners complain about.
>
> **How to teach it (simplified):**
> 1. Put a dog bed or mat on the floor
> 2. Lure your dog onto it with a treat
> 3. The moment all 4 paws are on, say "place" and reward
> 4. Release with "free" after 5 seconds
> 5. Repeat. Gradually increase duration: 10 seconds, 30, 1 minute, 5 minutes
>
> Within a week, most dogs will hold "place" for 30+ minutes. Guests come over? "Place." Cooking dinner? "Place." Doorbell rings? "Place."
>
> We teach this on day one of every program. It's that important.
