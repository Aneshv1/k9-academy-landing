## Why in-home training works better for certain dogs

Most dog training happens at a facility. Your dog learns to sit, stay, and recall in a controlled environment. Then you get home and it all falls apart.

That's not because your dog is dumb. It's because dogs are context learners. They associate commands with the place they learned them. Change the environment and the training doesn't transfer.

In-home training skips that problem entirely. Your dog learns in the exact environment where it needs to perform.

This matters most for:

- **Doorbell reactivity.** Your dog goes insane when someone rings the bell. You can't replicate that at a training facility.
- **Separation anxiety.** Your dog panics when you leave. That only happens at home.
- **Aggression with guests.** Your dog lunges at anyone who walks through the front door. We need to be at that front door.
- **Resource guarding.** Your dog guards the couch, the food bowl, the bed. Those are all at your house.

If your dog's issues are location-specific, in-home training is the fastest path to fixing them.
