## The Bridle Path requires a different kind of trainer.

Properties on The Bridle Path aren't typical Toronto homes. They're estates. Multi-acre lots, gated entrances, long driveways. The dogs that live there aren't typical either. Working breeds, large breeds, protection dogs, high-drive animals that need more than a basic obedience class.

Group training at a facility doesn't make sense for Bridle Path families. The environment is completely different from where the dog actually lives. And frankly, most Bridle Path residents aren't interested in sitting in a circle with 15 other dog owners at a community centre.

That's why we offer private in-home training.

## How it works

One of our senior trainers drives to your Bridle Path property. We work with your dog on your grounds, in your home, around your specific triggers and environment.

**What we typically address on Bridle Path properties:**

- Gate and perimeter reactivity. Dogs that lose it when someone approaches the gate, rings the intercom, or pulls into the driveway.
- Guest aggression. Large properties mean infrequent visitors, which means dogs aren't socialized to handle them well.
- Off-leash estate control. Your dog should be able to roam your property reliably without bolting or ignoring commands.
- Walks on Bridle Path and Post Road. Other dogs, joggers, cyclists. Your dog should handle all of it calmly.
