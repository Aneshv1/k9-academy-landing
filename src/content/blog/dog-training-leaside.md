## Why your dog's training location matters

Most people choose a dog trainer based on reviews, price, and availability. Almost nobody thinks about location — and it's one of the most important factors in whether training actually sticks.

Here's why: a dog trained in a quiet suburban facility an hour from your home learns to behave in that quiet suburban facility. When they come back to your Leaside sidewalk — with the streetcars, the off-leash dogs in Trace Manes Park, the cyclists on Bayview, the squirrels in Serena Gundy — they're in a completely different environment. And dogs don't generalize well.

Training needs to happen in the environment where your dog will live. That's why location matters.

## What makes training in your neighbourhood different

### Real-world proofing from day one

When we train your dog at our Leaside location, the distractions are real. Other dogs walking past the training area. Cars honking. People jogging. Construction noise. Your dog doesn't learn in a sterile, quiet room and then struggle when they step outside — they learn in the chaos and get comfortable in it.

### The walks transfer immediately

One of the most common complaints we hear: "My dog was great at the training facility but falls apart on our walks." This happens when dogs are trained in environments that don't match their daily life.

When your dog trains in Leaside — on the same sidewalks, past the same parks, around the same triggers — the skills transfer immediately. The walk home from training looks like training.

### Socialization with neighbourhood dogs

Your dog needs to be comfortable around the dogs they'll see every day. Not hypothetical dogs in a controlled setting — the actual golden retriever on Millwood, the reactive German shepherd on Laird, the off-leash poodle in Trace Manes. Training in the neighbourhood means your dog builds real tolerance for real triggers.
