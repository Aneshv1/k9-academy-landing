## Your local options

**Big box store classes:** PetSmart on Yonge Street and other locations offer classes for $150 to $200. Six weeks of basic sit, down, stay. The instructors rotate frequently, and the training is cookie-cutter. Good for socialization only.

**Independent local trainers:** Richmond Hill has a few independent trainers charging $200 to $400 for group programs. Some are solid for basic obedience. Ask about their experience with your specific issue before committing.

**Daycare-based training:** Some Richmond Hill daycares add training to their daycare package. This is usually a few minutes of obedience practice mixed into a play day. It's not structured training, and results are minimal.

## Richmond Hill parks for training practice

One of the best parts of living in Richmond Hill is the green space. Here are top spots to practice training:

**Mill Pond Park:** Right in the heart of Richmond Hill. Paved paths, water features, and moderate foot traffic. Great for leash manners and focus work around distractions.

**Phyllis Rawlinson Park:** A hidden gem with trails through wetlands and forest. Lower traffic than most Richmond Hill parks. Perfect for long-line recall practice.

**Richmond Green Sports Centre and Park:** Lots of open space and people. Good for proofing commands around moderate to high distractions. The large open fields work well for distance recall on a long line.

**Lake Wilcox Park:** Beautiful lakeside trails. Busier on weekends, which is great for advanced distraction training. Work on heel, recall, and place commands with real-world distractions.

**Oak Ridges Trail:** For the more adventurous. Longer trails with wildlife distractions. Once your dog has solid off-leash skills, this is where you get to enjoy the payoff.
