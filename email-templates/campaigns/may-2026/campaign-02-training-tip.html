You're accidentally poisoning their recall

"MAX! NO! MAX, STOP IT! MAX!"

Sound familiar? Here's the problem: every time you say your dog's name in a frustrated or angry tone, you're teaching them that their name means something bad is about to happen.

Then you go to the park, say "Max, come!" — and they ignore you. Not because they're stubborn. Because you've trained them to associate their name with punishment.

The fix:

1. Stop using their name for corrections. Use "no" or "eh-eh" instead.

2. For 2 weeks, say their name ONLY when something good follows — a treat, a pet, dinner.

3. Watch how fast their recall improves.

This is one of the first things we teach in every program.
